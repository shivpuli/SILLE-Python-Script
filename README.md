SILLE stands for Student Instrucionally Led Liquid Engine, and it was started by CPSS as a way for students of all levels and majors to learn how liquid engines work while 
desigining their own. CPSS would provide the materials and guide students during the process, but in the end, the engines would be calculated, designed, and machined by students
themselves. The last step of the process is to integrate the finished engine to one of the various test stands in CPSS's inventory and fire their very own liquid rocket engine.

This python script, when used in tandem with NASA's Rocket CEA Calculator, will produce chamber and ox/fuel orifice dimensions. It also creates a graphic and summary box to 
visualize the dimensions as you iterate your design.
